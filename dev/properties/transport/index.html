Transport Coefficients

Transport coefficients are expressed as matrix elements of the inverse collision operator under the weighted inner product:

\[X_{ij} = \langle \phi_i | L^{-1} | \phi_j \rangle \equiv \sum_{\mathbf{k}} f^{(0)}_{\mathbf{k}} (1 - f^{(0)}_{\mathbf{k}}) \, \Delta V_{\mathbf{k}} \, \phi^{(i)}_{\mathbf{k}} \, [L^{-1} \phi^{(j)}]_{\mathbf{k}}.\]

The driving vectors $\phi$ differ by transport channel: $\phi = v_i$ for charge transport, $\phi = \varepsilon v_i$ for heat transport. Numerically, $L^{-1} \phi$ is computed by solving the linear system $L \psi = \phi$ via a biconjugate gradient iteration.

Viscosity

For two-dimensional materials with tetragonal symmetry, the shear viscosity decomposes into B1g and B2g irreducible representations of the D4h point group. These are computed from the corresponding deformation potentials $D(\mathbf{k})$, which encode the coupling of the quasiparticle momentum to the relevant strain mode. See ηB1g and ηB2g.
